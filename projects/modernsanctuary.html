Modern Sanctuary

Fine Art Mixed media Extended investigation

When?

February - November 2020


Where?

Auckland, New Zealand


Why?

CIE AS Level Art & Design (Fine Art Painting; awarded Top in New Zealand) and NZQA Scholarship Painting. Plus a heightened desire of escapism during NZ's first COVID lockdowns.

This project explores the feeling of safety and the comfort one may receive from being present in an ideal environment, and investigates how we emotionally interact with these places of comfort.


There are two parallel sides to this concept: one is the regularity of our daily rituals and routines, and how they bring a sense of rhythm to our lives to keep us grounded (especially within the context of a global pandemic). Whether it be the steady splashing of tap water filling up a cup or the soft rustle of fresh fabric, the calm that accompanies them is like no other.

Part II: The Peach Blossom Spring


The Peach Blossom Spring (Ê°ÉËä±Ê∫êË®ò) is a traditional Chinese fable that tells the story of a fisherman accidentally discovering an ethereal utopia. Beyond an inconspicuous grotto at the end of a stream, The Peach Blossom Spring is situated in the middle of a forest of blossoming peach trees. The inhabitants there lead a peaceful, idyllic existence separated from the outside world. They welcome the fisherman warmly. Upon his departure, they remind him not to reveal his experience to others. However, the fisherman does not keep this promise. The ones who learn of his experience repeatedly try to find the Peach Blossom Spring, but their attempts are always in vain.


In Chinese culture, the tale of the Peach Blossom Spring symbolises a sanctuary and an ideal world, often being used as a metaphor in daily language.
