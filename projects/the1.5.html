The 1.5

Graphic design Adobe CC Extended investigation

When?

October - November 2021


Where?

Auckland, New Zealand


Why?

A very last minute submission for NZQA Scholarship (awarded Outstanding and Top Subject Scholar for Design)

As someone who immigrated from China to New Zealand at five years old, I am part of what I refer to as the "1.5 generation" — those who immigrated as children or youth.


The 1.5 is an idealised exhibition and cultural festival celebrating the identity of 1.5 generation Chinese-Kiwis. It aims to showcase individual stories from the local Auckland community, which as a region has by far the largest Chinese population in NZ with an abundance of recent migrants.


For this project, I designed collateral for the event alongside event branding. This included a social media graphic advertising story submissions, a website, and a take home zine for attendees.


The initial identity that I envisioned for this project features textural, neutral toned photography highlighted against more colourful & youthful graphical elements. A set colour palette drawing inspiration from both Chinese culture and the New Zealand landscape is used throughout the project’s graphical elements for continuity. Soft diffusions and gradients are used to visually express blurred boundaries and the idea of being "in-between". On top of this, layering and collage are also explored as a way of piecing together the two cultures.

Exploring diffusions and layering as a visual expression of "in-between" identities (spanning both cultures and generations!)
