Coral Sleeve

Biodesign Speculative design Textiles

When?

March 2023


Where?

Providence, Rhode Island


Why?

For RISD Foundation Studio: Spatial Dynamics (Spring 2022) with Katia Zolotovsky.

A freehand crochet sleeve that has the ability to expand and contract, when faced with extreme changes in temperature.


When asked to design a nature-inspired wearable that allows for human adaptation to a new environment, I decided to begin by studying the structure of organisms within a coral reef. At RISDâ€™s Nature Lab, I examined samples of fan coral and Venusâ€™ flower baskets, and specifically how their porous structures control the flow of water and nutrients.


Applying this method of fluid control to air, the potential arose to reimagine these structures as variable/adjustable insulation. Hence, I decided to design for an environment of rapid temperature fluctuations - perhaps not too unrealistic in a future of climate change.


The structure of the final prototype takes inspiration from the skeleton of a fan coral, and the expansion and contraction of Venusâ€™ flower baskets: expand and allow more air to flow through in warmer temperatures, contract and allow less air to flow through in cooler temperatures. It features seven panels of freehand crochet with double crocheted ribbon channels, taking approximately 30 hours to complete.
