Memory Study

Fine Art Mixed media Extended investigation

When?

February - November 2021


Where?

Auckland, New Zealand


Why?

CIE A Level Art & Design (Fine Art Painting) and NZQA Scholarship Painting. Fueled by a sense of indescribable urgency upon the dawn of the Stop Asian Hate movement.

Here, I aim to unpack the experience of growing up Chinese in Aotearoa New Zealand, drawing inspiration from both personal experience and researching the history of those who came before me.


In November 2008, my family arrived in Auckland, New Zealand from Shenzhen, China. Having spent the first five years of my life in between densely packed high rises, I remember being surprised at how much space there was in between the houses that lined the green suburban roadsides. The contrast in our living conditions and lifestyle were vast, such as my parents having to learn how to mow the lawn for the first time.


Growing up in between two seemingly disparate cultures has shaped my identity in unique ways. This body of work is an attempt to translate memory and history into tangible subject matter, and to rediscover my identity in this diverse and ever-changing narrative.
